The Cause

The suffragettes utilized destructive tactics and negative press to bring attention to the rights of women and the right to vote. The suffragettes destroyed mailboxes, telephone wires, abandoned buildings, and the homes of Political leaders. In the early 1900s, all of the tactics used by the suffragettes were used to bring attention towards a woman's right to vote.

Negative press is exactly how the Suffragettes got their name. Charles E. Hands of the Daily Mail coined the name Suffragettes and used it in a condescending way to make fun of these women. However, being brilliant campaign Artists, they used the negative press so well that they even named their own newspaper The Suffragette. Taking Charles's words, and his power away from him and turning it into power for women.


The suffragettes stemmed from the suffragists. The suffragists were peaceful protesters fighting for Women's rights and the right to vote. The suffragettes took a more hands-on attention-based approach to the problem. When the suffragettes gained attention and pulled stunts, they were able to say people care about The Cause and bring attention to the men in power withholding the vote.
